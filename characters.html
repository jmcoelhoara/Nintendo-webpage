Donkey Kong is a classic video game created by Nintendo and released in 1981. Designed by Shigeru Miyamoto, the game introduced two iconic characters: Donkey Kong, a giant ape, and Mario, originally called Jumpman. In the game, players control Mario as he attempts to rescue a damsel in distress, Pauline, by navigating platforms, climbing ladders, and avoiding obstacles thrown by Donkey Kong. The game was a major success, becoming one of the best-selling arcade machines of the early 1980s and establishing Nintendo as a key player in the video game industry.

It also spawned sequels like Donkey Kong Jr. and Donkey Kong 3, and later a reboot with the Donkey Kong Country series in 1994, which revitalized the franchise. Donkey Kong has had a lasting cultural impact, influencing video games and pop culture for decades. Its legacy continues to thrive, with Donkey Kong appearing in various other Nintendo games and remaining a beloved character in gaming history.

Mario and Luigi are not just the faces of Nintendo but also represent the broader video game industry. Their games have been lauded for their creativity, gameplay, and appeal to players of all ages, securing their place as two of the most beloved characters in entertainment history. Mario debuted in Donkey Kong (1981) as "Jumpman," a carpenter, and was later rebranded as a plumber. Luigi, Mario's younger brother, was introduced in Mario Bros. (1983) as his green-clad sidekick.

Their breakthrough came with Super Mario Bros. (1985) on the NES, where they teamed up to rescue Princess Peach from Bowser. This game established them as stars of the platforming genre. Mario and Luigi have since appeared in countless sequels and spin-offs, including the Mario Kart series and Luigi's Mansion. They've also become cultural icons, featuring in TV shows, movies, and merchandise, remaining central to Nintendo's legacy and beloved by fans worldwide.
